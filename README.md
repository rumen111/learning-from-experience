\# Learning from Experience



This project studies sequential decision-making under uncertainty and how different strategies perform in terms of optimizing payoffs.



The following three strategies are evaluated:



\- Greedy strategy

\- Upper Confidence Bound (UCB)

\- Thompson Sampling



We simplify the environment to a decision between a safe option and a risky option. The probabilities and outcomes are not explicitly known, but can be learned through choices. We consider both a short and long time horizon and vary the noise of the risky option. We run Monte Carlo simulations to compare the average cumulative rewards generated by the strategies.
